The Story

When letters became a nation’s voice

The Vision

Mesrop Mashtots saw that a nation needed its own written language to preserve knowledge, prayer, law, and education.

The Mission

The alphabet became more than writing. It became a bridge between generations and a shield for Armenian culture.

The Legacy

Through manuscripts, schools, churches, and books, the Armenian letters continued to carry memory across time.

“To know wisdom and instruction, to perceive the words of understanding.”

The Alphabet

Thirty-six immortal letters

The original Armenian alphabet contained 36 letters. Each letter became part of a sacred cultural architecture: language, literature, identity, and survival.
