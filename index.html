The Story

When letters became
a nation’s voice

Chapter I

The Vision

Mesrop Mashtots understood that a nation needs its own written language to preserve knowledge, prayer, law, and education. Without letters of its own, faith and culture would dissolve into borrowed tongues.

Chapter II

The Mission

The alphabet became more than writing. It became a bridge between generations and a shield for Armenian culture — forged through divine inspiration, scholarship, and a people’s longing to be heard across time.

Chapter III

The Legacy

Through manuscripts, schools, churches, and books, the Armenian letters carried memory across centuries of conquest, diaspora, and survival. Thirty-six letters — immortal and enduring.

The Alphabet

Thirty-six
immortal letters

The original Armenian alphabet contained 36 letters, created by Mesrop Mashtots in 405 AD. Each letter was designed to represent the unique sounds of the Armenian language — a sacred architecture of language, literature, identity, and survival.
